## 1. Hero Copy Evaluation

### Current Copy

**Badge:** "AI-Powered UX Design"
**Headline:** "UX Design. AI Speed. Real Results."
**Sub-headline:** "I help startups and growth-stage companies ship better products faster — using AI-accelerated design workflows that cut delivery time without cutting quality."
**Stat cards:** "40% faster delivery" · "20+ projects shipped" · "5.0★ average client rating on Upwork"

### Assessment

**Badge:** Violates brand language guidelines. "AI-Powered" is anti-vocabulary (product-demo framing). Change to "AI-accelerated design" or "Available for new projects."

**Headline:** Functional but generic. The three-fragment structure ("X. X. X.") is visually strong and should be kept. But the content could apply to any AI design tool or SaaS product — it says nothing specifically about Harsha. The word "Real" is doing too much work as a differentiator.

**Sub-headline:** Mostly good. "Startups and growth-stage companies" correctly reflects the 90-day audience priority — the absence of Enterprise is acceptable here. "Cut delivery time without cutting quality" is the strongest clause; "ship better products faster" is slightly redundant given "cut delivery time." One clause ("AI-accelerated design workflows") is tool-framing rather than outcome-framing.

**Stat cards:** All three are confirmed aspirational/placeholder. "40% faster" and "20+ projects" are unverifiable estimates. "5.0★ Upwork" is a credibility claim the visitor can check — if no Upwork profile is linked, this actively hurts trust. All three must be replaced or removed before launch.

### Proposed Improved Version

**Badge:** "Available for new projects" *(simple, honest, conversion-focused)*

**Headline:** (keep three-fragment structure, sharpen the middle)
```
UX Design.
AI Speed.
Real Results.
```
This headline works once the stat cards are real and the case studies are real. The problem is not the headline itself — it's that the supporting proof doesn't yet exist. Prioritise replacing the stats and case studies before changing the headline.

**Alternative headline if stats remain unverifiable:**
```
UX Design.
Faster.
Yours.
```
More confident and personal. Less dependent on proof. But loses the AI angle — only worth considering if the AI positioning is deprioritised.

**Sub-headline (improved):**
> "I help product teams at startups and growth-stage companies ship better work, faster — using AI-assisted workflows that compress timelines without compressing thinking."

Changes: "AI-accelerated design workflows" → "AI-assisted workflows" (less jargon); "cut delivery time without cutting quality" → "compress timelines without compressing thinking" (more distinctive, avoids the word "quality").

**Stat cards — proposed replacements once real data exists:**
- Replace "40% faster delivery" with a specific project example: "8-week design system that replaced a 6-month agency quote"
- Replace "20+ projects shipped" with "9 years of product design" (real, verifiable, Harsha confirmed)
- Replace "5.0★ Upwork" with the real Upwork rating once profile is live and linked

**Startup:** "Get investor-ready, user-validated design — without hiring a full team."
- Assessment: **Strong.** Client-language. Speaks to the exact fear (cost of a full team) and the exact desire (investor-ready, user-validated). Keep with minor punctuation check.

**Growth:** "Build the design infrastructure that lets your team ship faster."
- Assessment: Leads with solution ("build") rather than the problem ("your team is a bottleneck"). The brand language guide Example 3 already documents this. Improved version leads with the pain.
- Improved: "Your design is a bottleneck. We'll fix that — and make sure it doesn't come back."
- Or more direct: "Stop rebuilding the same components. Build a design system that scales."

**Enterprise:** "Design leadership that handles stakeholder complexity and compliance."
- Assessment: Good. Addresses the real enterprise pain (stakeholder complexity) specifically. "Compliance" is the right word for this audience.

## 7. Case Study Copy Standards

A field-by-field template for Harsha to follow when providing real project details.

### Title
**Format:** [Outcome or transformation] for [company type or product type]
**Not:** "Redesigning the X experience" (process-led) or "X App Redesign" (deliverable-led)
**Good examples:** "Design system that unified 3 platforms in 8 weeks" · "Onboarding redesign that reduced drop-off by 40%"
**Rule:** The title should make a non-designer want to keep reading.

### Description (homepage card + project hero — 1–2 sentences)
**Format:** State the problem + the result. No design jargon.
**Template:** "[Business context/problem]. [What changed as a result of the design work]."
**Not:** "I designed a new onboarding flow with improved UX patterns."
**Good:** "New users were dropping off in the first 5 minutes. A redesigned onboarding cut that drop-off by 40%."

### Problem Statement (full paragraph)
**Format:** Business problem first, user problem second, consequence of inaction third.
**Template:** "[The company] was facing [specific business problem]. [Users were experiencing specific friction]. Without addressing this, [business consequence]."
**Rule:** Write it so a non-designer (a CEO, a VP of Product) immediately understands why this mattered.
**Anti-pattern:** "The existing UI was outdated and lacked consistency." (design-language, not business-language)

### Solution Summary (full paragraph)
**Format:** Decision rationale first, approach second, key choices made third.
**Template:** "Rather than [obvious/expected approach], we [the actual approach taken]. This meant [key design principle or constraint]. The critical decisions were [1–2 specific choices and why they were made]."
**Rule:** Show thinking, not just output. A hiring client wants to see how you reason, not just what you shipped.

### Outcomes (array of 4 items)
**Format:** [Number + unit] [what improved / what changed]
**Template:** "X% [metric that a non-designer understands]"
**Good:** "40% reduction in onboarding drop-off" · "8-week delivery vs. 6-month agency quote" · "WCAG AA compliance achieved across all 3 platforms"
**Not:** "Improved user experience" · "Better design system coverage" · "Enhanced usability"
**Rule:** Every outcome must be a number or a before/after comparison. No outcome should require design knowledge to understand.

### AI Workflow (4 phases)
**Format per phase:** Phase name | Tool used | Outcome/impact in client language
**Rule:** Lead with what the AI workflow meant for the client (speed, cost, quality), not what tool was used. The tool is secondary.
**Good:** "Discovery phase: Used Otter.ai + Claude to synthesise 12 user interviews in 2 days instead of 2 weeks. Client saw findings faster, made decisions sooner."
**Not:** "Used Claude and GPT-4 for synthesis."

### Process Timeline (4 phases)
**Format:** Phase name | Duration | 1-sentence description of what happened and what it produced
**Rule:** End each phase description with what the client received or decided at that stage. Process is only interesting in terms of what it unlocked.
