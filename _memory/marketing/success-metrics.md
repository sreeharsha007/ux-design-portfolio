# Success Metrics — Harsha's UX Portfolio

Marketing Director output. First run, April 2026.
No analytics baseline exists. These are forward-looking targets and a measurement framework.

---

## Primary Conversion Metric

**Inbound project inquiry via email or Upwork.**

A visitor reads the site, decides Harsha is qualified and credible, and initiates contact. Everything else on this site — every section, every stat, every case study — is in service of producing that one action.

Secondary conversion actions (LinkedIn follow, Upwork profile visit) are proxies for intent, not conversions.

---

## Supporting Metrics

These are signals of portfolio health and pipeline strength, in descending order of importance.

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|--------|---------------|
| Qualified inbound inquiries per month | Volume of potential projects entering the funnel |
| Inquiry-to-proposal rate | Site is attracting the right people (quality filter) |
| Proposal-to-project rate | Downstream of the site — reflects pricing and fit |
| Upwork profile visits from site | Primary acquisition channel actually being reached |
| Contact page scroll-through rate | Are visitors reaching the CTA at all? |
| Case study page views | Indicates research intent — visitors evaluating credibility |
| Average session depth (pages viewed) | Proxy for visitor engagement and interest level |
| LinkedIn connection growth (post-launch) | Brand visibility in the target professional network |

## 30 / 90 / 180-Day Targets

These are calibrated to Harsha's stated goals: not chasing volume, quality of projects matters. The site is currently a supporting channel (primary is Upwork), with the ambition to make it a standalone channel post-MVP.

### 30 Days (post-launch)
- Site is live with real case studies and Harsha's name throughout
- Google Analytics or equivalent is installed and collecting data
- Upwork profile link is live and functional
- At least 1 inbound inquiry attributed to the site
- Email contact link is confirmed functional

### 90 Days
- 3–5 qualified inbound inquiries per month
- At least 2 active proposals in-flight sourced from direct inbound or site-supported Upwork leads
- At least 1 project closed in the $5k–$15k range (current target tier)
- At least 1 real testimonial collected and published to replace a placeholder
- Sufficient traffic data to identify top-performing and drop-off pages

### 180 Days
- 5–8 qualified inbound inquiries per month (site functioning as a standalone channel)
- At least 1 inquiry in the $15k–$50k range (6-month target tier)
- All 4 case studies populated with real content
- All 3 testimonials real
- Average project value trending upward — site framing attracting growth-stage clients as intended

## What "Success" Looks Like in Plain Language

**At 30 days:** The site is not embarrassing. Harsha's name is on it, the links work, there's at least one real project, and you could share it with a prospective client without wincing.

**At 90 days:** The site is actively generating leads. Harsha is mentioning it in Upwork proposals as a credibility link, and occasionally a client reaches out directly without being sourced from Upwork first.

**At 180 days:** The site is a standalone acquisition tool. At least one $15k+ project can be traced back to the site either directly or as a meaningful part of the closing sequence. Harsha is no longer relying entirely on Upwork for lead generation.

## Measurement Plan

Given no analytics exists yet, the following is the minimal viable measurement setup.

### Immediate (before launch)
1. Install Google Analytics 4 (free, no technical skill required once the tracking code is added). Configure:
   - A goal event for "Contact CTA click" (the email button and Upwork link)
   - A goal event for "Case study page view"
2. Set up Google Search Console (free) — connects the site to search visibility data.
3. Create a simple spreadsheet (or Notion table) to manually log every inbound inquiry: date, source (how they found Harsha), project type, budget range, and outcome.

### Ongoing (monthly review)
- Review Google Analytics: top pages, session duration, drop-off pages, and goal completions.
- Review the inquiry log: count leads, note source, note project tier, note outcome.
- Review Upwork analytics: profile views, invite rate, and whether the portfolio link is being visited from proposals.
- Ask every new client a simple question: "How did you find me?" Log the answer.

### No-cost, no-code approach
If adding Google Analytics to the site is too technical at launch, a simpler interim method:
- Use a dedicated email address (e.g. hello@[domain].com) only linked from the portfolio — any message to that address is a direct attribution.
- Use a unique Calendly link only published on the portfolio page — any booking there is a direct attribution.

## Note on Current Metrics Display

The site currently displays three headline stats in the hero:

- **"40% faster delivery vs. traditional UX process"** — aspirational, not data-backed
- **"20+ projects shipped across SaaS, FinTech & HealthTech"** — aspirational
- **"5.0★ average client rating on Upwork"** — aspirational, no active Upwork profile yet

And three secondary stats in the AI Workflow Stack section:

- **"4× faster research synthesis"** (3–5 days vs. 2–4 weeks)
- **"3× more concepts per project"**
- **"50% less handoff friction"**

### Recommendation: Replace hero stats; keep workflow stats with qualification

**Hero stats (40%, 20+, 5.0★):** These should not go live as-is. Displaying unverified quantitative claims on a professional portfolio is a credibility risk — especially if a prospective client asks Harsha to back them up in a discovery call. The risk is not legal, it is trust-based. If a client later discovers these were aspirational, it undermines the entire relationship.

**Options, in order of preference:**

1. **Best option — Replace with real data once it exists.** The structure of the stat cards is excellent. Keep them exactly as-is visually, but populate with real numbers. Even modest real numbers (e.g. "8 projects shipped", "4.9★ on Upwork from 3 reviews") outperform impressive fake numbers in conversion.

2. **Interim option — Reframe as process claims, not performance claims.** Replace the three cards with qualitative differentiators that cannot be falsified: e.g. "Full lifecycle — research to dev handoff", "AI embedded in every phase", "Works across SaaS, FinTech & HealthTech". Less powerful than metrics, but credible.

3. **Do not keep the current aspirational stats.** The risk outweighs any conversion upside.

**Workflow stats (4×, 3×, 50%):** These are more defensible because they describe the *process mechanics* (e.g. how long a phase takes with vs. without AI) rather than Harsha's track record. They can be kept if framed carefully — e.g. "Research synthesis in 3–5 days, not 2–4 weeks" is a process claim, not a client outcome claim. However, they should still be qualified with context (e.g. "based on [X methodology]") to avoid presenting them as independently verified statistics.
